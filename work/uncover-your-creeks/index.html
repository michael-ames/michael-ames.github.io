Uncover Your Creeks

Government and citizen scientists protecting urban watersheds

Vancouver, BC
2012-2015

Role(s):
management

The national urban environmental non-profit organization Evergreen launched a three-year project to build an urban watershed network, connecting municipal and regional governments, researchers, allied non-profit organizations, and volunteer citizen scientists.

With waterways so central to the ecology of the urban pacific northwest, the region needed better water quality data-sharing, more volunteer-supported data collection, and increased lay participation in local watershed planning.

The three-year project:

  • Established volunteer citizen scientist teams in seven area watersheds
  • Convened a working group of researchers to collaborate on a regional data-sharing platform
  • Partnered with city staff to increase turnout at municipal watershed planning consultation sessions
  • Hosted an annual Urban Watershed Forum
  • Trained corporate employees in water quality monitoring as part of an organizational sustainability program
  • Supported the work of other non-profits through joint fundraising
