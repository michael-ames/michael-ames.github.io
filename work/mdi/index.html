Discover MDI

Sharing public-health data to support child well-being

Vancouver, BC
2018
Role: Digital Community Lead

The Human Early Learning Partnership (HELP) at the University of British Columbia collects and disseminates population-level data on the well-being of children through the Middle Years Development Instrument (MDI). To increase uptake and support end-users’ understanding of these datasets, I worked with HELP to build its first online learning platform, Discover MDI.

After reviewing insights collected through focus groups and interviews, I designed and launched a pilot website aimed at HELP’s target audiences: educators, healthcare workers, and local and regional government employees. This website underwent a redesign several months later following additional rounds of user testing and interviews.

  • Language used on DiscoverMDI avoids jargon and is simpler than HELP’s existing materials designed for a researcher audience. I worked with HELP’s graphic designers to create new infographics and resources to support our primary users in communicating with critical secondary audiences (families, students, and staff).

  • I created the MDI Champions program to celebrate the ingenuity and dedication of users and to support peer learning across the province. Users wishing to share their stories and expertise with others receive profiles on the website and serve as “guest stars” on monthly MDI network training webinars.

  • A full set of workshop materials (facilitation guides, activities, presentation slides) were unveiled in 2018 to further support communities in using MDI data in their change efforts. Initial user testing was conducted in the spring, followed by a full-scale pilot.
